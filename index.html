About Me

My journey as a developer started during the time when making custom blackberry apps and setting up blogger was a trend.

I will be graduating with a Computer Science degree in December 2018 from Stony Brook University.

My most recent experience was an internship at Atlassian Inc. where I worked with the Trello team. I worked on mitigating the effects of spam through the use of SparkPost APIs and token bucket algorithm. I was also exposed to Naive Bayes classifier for assigning spamminess score to text strings. Technologies I worked with includes MongoDB, NodeJS and BackboneJS.

Before that, I worked with Zebra Technologies as a Developer Intern. I employed the the Robot Testing Framework to automate tests that detects Android OS hardware and software issues. I worked with Python, ADB, UI Automator, TCP/IP and UDP connections.

Before that I have been involved in doing research work on assistive technology. I and a team of awesome people created an assistive learning application that help visually impaired users to navigate indoor systems. I worked with C#, Unity 3D, Java and Android Studio. We published a paper on this application in the Journal on Technology and Persons with Disabilities CSUN at the CSUN Assistive Technology Conference, California.
